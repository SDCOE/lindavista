Feedback from SDCOE’s Community Forums Around Linda Vista Site

The Innovation Division of the San Diego County Office of Education (SDCOE) held 4 community planning meetings between late January and early March, 2020 to gather input and strategic thinking about how to use our new facility in Linda Vista.  

Overall, about 80 attendees ranging from students to Linda Vista area community leaders to local educators participated in these convenings. The following heat map visual displays the summary interests for our new facility’s use across the various groups meeting in these convenings.   

Groups universally expressed interest in tying the facility to innovative programs and services collaborating with business and community to emphasize career and job training, STEM and industry labs and after school programs. All groups have an interest in involving the whole Linda Vista community in services. Students also emphasized an interest in art/dance, clubs, and fitness.

Feedback from SDCOE World Café Convening

SDCOE Comstock Building, 1/23/2020

The Innovation Division of the San Diego County Office of Education held a community planning meeting last month to gather input and strategic thinking about how to use our new facility in Linda Vista. Below is a summary of results from the meeting.

What populations do we serve?

The groups that SDCOE could serve in the Linda Vista community are wide-ranging from early education to supports for students in underserved communities to succeed in school through CTE and transitions to higher education and the workforce.


Feedback on what SDCOE does well, could improve

What SDCOE does well can be grouped into these categories:
• Services (HR, credentials, content/analysis and technology);
• Connecting people and fostering innovation;
• Event support and meeting space;
• Training and professional development; and
• Providing resources to school districts. 

Participants feel that SDCOE could improve in these areas:
• Foster and facilitate dialog in the community and partnerships
• Create (STEM, CTE, business, work) opportunities for all
students, educators and the local economy
• Provide curriculum and be more active in the schools and
classrooms and bring back well-liked programs
• Build community college partnerships
• Provide services to support parents, families

Innovation Network Meeting 1

SDCOE Comstock Building, 2/28/2020

This convening was attended by 20 area school and district leaders and took place in the multipurpose room at the Linda Vista Comstock building.

After introductions and a tour of the facility, questions were presented for discussion and brainstorming activities.

Questions included:
• If you were going to redesign a school system from the ground up - what would it look like?
• What are your thoughts, ideas and hopes for Linda Vista site and partnerships?

Attendees worked in groups discussing the questions and writing ideas on large posters (see adjacent).

After the meeting, SDCOE staff transcribed the poster contents and listed out the themes discusses at the convening, summarizing the number of times each theme appeared into a final tally visual.

Innovation Network Meeting 2

North County Regional Education Center, 3/2/2020 

What populations do we serve?

This convening was attended by 15 north county San Diego school and district leaders at the North County Regional Education Center (NECREC). At the North County meeting, the brainstorming activities were centered around these questions: 

• Equity: Giving students what they need to achieve equally high outcomes

• Think Big > Think Bigger – sharing big ideas & thoughts around our new site and potential uses

• Combine our strengths and create a group idea that will benefit schools and kids

Attendees wrote their ideas on large posters (see right).  After the meeting, SDCOE staff transcribed the poster contents and listed out the themes discusses at the convening, summarizing the number of times each theme appeared into a final tally visual.

Student Forum - Linda Vista Area Students

SDCOE Comstock Building, 3/6/2020

Attendees for this convening were 20 students from Kearny H.S. Montgomery M.S, Empower Charter other area schools. Students received a briefing on the meeting, then toured the site, after which they discussed their ideas for the building. During the discussion and brainstorming session, attendees were broken down in groups of 3-4 and wrote their ideas on large posters.   

After the meeting, SDCOE staff transcribed the poster contents and listed out the themes discusses at the convening, summarizing the number of times each theme appeared into a final tally visual.
